# Position Timing: How To Calculate Length Of Position

**Module:** Smart Trading Techniques  
**Lesson:** 2 of 5

---

A lot of folks have differences of opinions when it comes to how long they like to hold positions (or what length positions they like to focus on). However, the length of your position can only be estimated, and thus you need to plan out beforehand. The market can change at the drop of a hat, and thus we recommend following some common sense rules when finding positions of certain lengths of time.

## Day Trading: (Buying & Selling Intraday)

- Rule #1: Your Position Is A DAY TRADE if the pattern, elevating factors, or confirmation that you are trading off of is on the 1day - 5 day chart

## Swing Trading: (Buying & Selling Over Multiple Days - Weeks)

- Rule #1: Your Position is A SWING TRADE if the pattern, elevating factors, or confirmation that you are trading off of is on the 5-day - 180 day chart

## Position Trading: (Buying & Selling Over Multiple Months - 1 Year)

- Rule #1: Your Position Is A POSITION TRADE if the pattern, elevating factors, or confirmation that you are trading off of is on the 180 day+ chart

---

## Examples:

**1.)** You see that NIO is oversold/overbought on the 180 day chart. You are seeing a price strength confirmation on that 180 day chart, and are considering taking a position. What type of position do you estimate this will be?

**Answer:** If it takes an average of three weeks to go from oversold to overbought, that means that your estimated position is going to take approximately three weeks to complete. That means that you are using the 180 day chart but you are planning on making a swing trade since your estimate is three weeks.

**2.)** You see that ROKU has strong elevating factors intraday, and is providing a confirmation on that same 1 minute 1 day chart. What type of position do you think this will be?

**Answer:** If these elevating factors are present on the intraday (1 minute, 1 day chart), then it makes sense for this to be a day trade.

**3.)** You see that NBRV is running up massively before an FDA announcement. You notice that the previous FDA announcements caused week-long run ups before them. You are planning to wait and buy in at confirmation, what type of position do you think this will be?

**Answer:** Since you are planning on holding for multiple days due to a pattern of running up for a week beforehand, it makes sense that this would be at least a multiple day position.

---

## Things To Note

If your position breaks or deviates from your trading plan, CUT IT LOOSE. You do not stay in a position just because you designated it as a certain period of time. If a swing trade drops 20% and validates you out on the same day you bought it, stick to your plan! Don't just hold and hope and let it keep running because you designated it as a swing trade.
