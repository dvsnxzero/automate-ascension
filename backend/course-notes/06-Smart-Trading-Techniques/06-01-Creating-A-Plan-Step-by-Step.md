# Creating A Plan: Step-by-Step

**Module:** Smart Trading Techniques  
**Lesson:** 1 of 5

---

How do you create a plan? Well, you need to find concrete entry and exit points and you need to execute on those REGARDLESS of what happens.

## Things to ask yourself..

### 1.) What needs to happen for me to enter a position

A. Am I Waiting For Confirmation?

B. What Elevating Factors Am I Waiting For?

- Is It A GOOD deal? Do we have a PATTERN confirmation? How much price strength do we have? Do we have a high probability setup?

### 2.) How Will I FIND OUT when or if the stock fits my plan?

A. Have I Set An Alert?

B. Am I Watching The Chart?

C. Did I Place A Limit Order?

### 3.) What needs to happen for me to exit a position?

A. Am I Waiting For Validation?

B. What Deprecating Factors Am I Waiting For?

- Is It now OVERBOUGHT? Do we have a BREAK of pattern? Are we losing price strength?

### 4.) How Will I FIND OUT when or if the stock fits the exit plan?

A. Have I Set An Alert?

B. Am I Watching The Chart?

C. Did I Place A Limit Order?

---

## Examples Of Good Entry Plans:

- "I am noticing that we are oversold, so I will buy in if we have a confirmation of price strength"
- "I am noticing that we have a previous pattern of going from $55 to $65 over and over again, so once we return to $55 and have a confirmation, I'll take a position"
- "This stock has had a pushback from an uptrend, I want to take advantage. But I'll wait until confirmation to do so"

## Examples Of Bad Entry Plans:

- "I notice that this stock is oversold, so I'll buy in because its discounted"
- "I am noticing that we have a previous pattern of going from $55 to $65, so I'll blindly buy in when it's dropping towards $55"
- "This stock has had a pushback from an uptrend, I want to take advantage NOW! I'll just buy in since we are in a pushback"

## Examples of Good EXIT Plans:

- "I am noticing that we are overbought, and we have dropping price strength, so I'll exit if hit validation"
- "There are a lot of deprecating factors, so I'll get out"

## Examples of Bad EXIT Plans:

- "I will hold and hope until the price action goes back up! I refuse to lose money."

---

## What Good Plans have in common..

A. They all involve a concrete entry (confirmation) & concrete exit (validation) point.

B. They all involve evaluating different elevating and deprecating factors at each confirmation & validation point. You don't JUST buy or sell based on confirm. & validat. points.

C. They are clear and easy to follow
