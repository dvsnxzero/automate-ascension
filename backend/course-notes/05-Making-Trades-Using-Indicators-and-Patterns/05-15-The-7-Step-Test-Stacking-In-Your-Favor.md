# The 7-Step Test: Stacking In Your Favor

**Module:** Making Trades Using Indicators & Patterns  
**Lesson:** 15 of 15

---

## 1.) Choose a Time Frame to FOCUS On

Which time frame are you going to be focusing on? Choose the one that holds the duration of your position AND holds the pattern/movement at which you seek to trade.

## 2.) Evaluate Your Risk vs. Reward

Do you have more upward potential as compared to downward potential? Is there enough reward "road" to drive the price action up?

## 3.) Check your Elevating and Deprecating Factors

Are you buying at a good deal? Do you have price strength? Are you in an upward direction? Do you have a confirmation of price strength?

## 4.) How does the position look long term?

What are the elevating and deprecating factors long term? If you have some serious deprecating factors long term, make sure that you note that when taking a position.

## 5.) Are there news catalysts?

Why am I trading the stock? Is it related to a news catalyst? Make sure you know that every reaction in the stock market is an overreaction, and thus price action is often temporarily distorted. But remember that this will work in your FAVOR and AGAINST you at different times.

## 6.) What is the analyst price target?

Look at where the analyst price target is. If it is much higher than your current trading price, that is a huge elevating factor as sheep investors love that. They will help bid up the price.

## 7.) Is the Position WORTH IT?

Do not trade a setup that doesn't make sense based on your elevating and deprecating factors. Overtrading is a disease and will eat through your account like a jack rabbit eating jack cheese. Make sure that you are trading like a SPOILED BRAT and only executing on the very best of setups.
