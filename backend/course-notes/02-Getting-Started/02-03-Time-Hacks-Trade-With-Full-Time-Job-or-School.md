# Time Hacks: Trade With Full-Time Job or School

**Module:** Getting Started  
**Lesson:** 3 of 4

---

**The Problem:** A lot of folks like to come up with excuses for why they cannot trade or even learn to trade. This is a legitimate concern - trading takes a LOT of time and consistent effort (especially as a beginner). However - this phenomenon is true for literally any skill that takes effort to succeed in. It is always going to be easy to say "I don't have time to accomplish 'a' because of 'b' reason."

**The Solution:** Stop Making Excuses. Analyze your time - and see where trading and trading education can fit in. I understand that you likely have a full-time job or are in school. I understand that you may have children or other really important family commitments. But if trading is something that is important to you - then you need to look at your schedule honestly and fit in SOME TIME.

**Cut out the Fat:** If you spend more than 1 hour per day doing something mindless (watching TV, playing on your phone, games, etc), you have NO RIGHT to say that you don't have time to invest in your education. Let's go ahead and put that time to work by employing it towards growing your financial future. The truth is, trading is a lot more engaging than mindless activities and you'll probably enjoy it more ANYWAYS.

**Clear Priorities:** We all have different levels of commitment and legitimate time constraints. However, you won't be able to accomplish much in life if you don't clearly prioritize what is important to you.

**My Hypocrisy:** The truth is, I'm a bit of a hypocrite. When I started with my stock market passion - I didn't have a ton of other time commitments. The only things I had to worry about were a part-time job and my coursework at a relaxed University (UCSC, mascot is the banana slug!). However, I noticed most people squandered their time with random social events, television watching, and computer surfing. Once I graduated, I quickly found that the same people worked 40-50 hours a week and then squandered the rest of their time as well. My point is, you may have more or less time than I had. But if learning is going to be something you want to accomplish - my guess is there is something that you can move around to MAKE time for it to be accomplished.

---

Let's get you some examples of different scenarios to give some ideas on how to find the time. Of course, each person is going to have a different life situation (and time zone) so apply accordingly. This is not meant to oversimplify your time commitments, but rather to give you ideas of how much time you can be spending to make progress.

## Examples of Learner Trading Schedules (Pacific Standard Time)

**A. 25 Year Old With 40 Hour Work Week**
- Trades Pre-Market & First Hour Of Market Open (Before Work)
- Studies and Researches Two-Three Hours Each Night
- Checks market routinely throughout break schedules

**B. 45 Year Old With 40 Hour Work Week & 3 Children**
- Gets up early and trades Pre-market & First 30 Minutes of Market Open
- Studies And Researches An Hour Each Night

**C. 65 Year Old Retired Individual**
- Gets up early and trades and most hours of the market
- Studies and researches when market is closed

**D. 18 Year Old With School Commitments**
- Gets up early and trades pre-market and first hours
- Checks market routinely and practice trades during time between classes
- Studies each and every night

## How Do I Trade If I Work During Market Hours?

If this is your issue, you are going to need to cater your positions to fit your time schedule. That might mean going more heavy on swing positions (multi-day or multi-week) and it'll mean A LOT of research the night before. You'll learn in this course how to find great positions, but keep in mind that if you are working on a longer time span, you just simply need to adjust the chart to fit the time span that fits your desired schedule.

Depending on your time zone, you'll also likely be able to trade in the pre-market or (for west coasters) the first hour or so of market open.

## How does this work in practice?

We have tons of folks who want to trade part-time (along with their regular job) and this actually works fantastically. The reason is because if you aren't stressed about making trading your sole income - you'll likely be more patient and only take the best setups. The key is making sure to manage your time and resources available to you in order to maximize your positions.
