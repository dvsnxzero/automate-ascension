## 3 Rules of Leveraged ETFs

### Rule 1) Don't Hold Overnight

Leveraged ETFs multiply the movement of the underlying ETF each day but they do not adjust well long term. Due to the multiplication effect, leveraged ETF's will suffer in the long run. Thus, they are adjusted at certain periods to account for this. But the longer you hold a leveraged ETF, the more pressure it has to go down. Thus it is extremely important to limit your use of leveraged ETFs to day trading (especially as a beginner).

### Rule 2) Trade the ETF partner that is in an upward direction

Always focus on trading the ETF pair that is in an upward direction. We want more elevating factors than deprecating ones, so it makes sense to focus on the pair that is in an upward direction. (Hint: It's easy to tell if a stock is in an upward direction, just check to see whether its above or below the directional SMA line)

### Rule 3) Make Sure To Identify Volatility To Trade Off Of

Leveraged ETFs were built for providing traders with volatility, so if the ETF you are interested in trading isn't showing sufficient moves (0.5%, bare minimum) or doesn't have trading volume - avoid the plague!
