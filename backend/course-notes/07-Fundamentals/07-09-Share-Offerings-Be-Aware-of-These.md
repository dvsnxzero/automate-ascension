# Share Offerings: Be Aware of These!

**Module:** Fundamentals: What They Tell Us & How To USE them  
**Lesson:** 9 of 12

---

As traders you will often comes across these two types of offerings, these are essentially the issuance of new shares. But the context of these offerings is everything.

## A. IPO (Initial Public Offering)

This is when a company first decides to go through the process of being publicly traded (and the first time we can trade it). Companies decide on an IPO price to introduce their stock to the market (usually it's much higher than it deserves to be). But there is often a lot of hype surrounding IPOs as people often flock to buy in during the first few days of open (and often expect to get a good deal as an early buyer). This can and often does result in huge fluctuations and hype opportunities. The issue with this though is that since we don't have any previous price action to evaluate the stock's behavior, it becomes extremely difficult to manage our risk. I strongly recommend waiting a few weeks to trade recent IPOs, that way you can evaluate the stock's behavior first.

Be aware that IPOs are very difficult to analyze!

## B. Direct Offerings

This is when companies that are already trading publicly decide to issue more shares (usually to raise more money). This is usually not a good thing for current shareholders as the issuance of new shares means that there are more shares available and that dilutes the value of the already available shares.

You can think of this as "share inflation," if you have the same root company but more shares available - you've just made each share already held worth less. But this isn't black and white, shares being added means more capital and that capital is going to be used for something. But most of the time, the issuance of new shares does not increase the value of the root company in proportion to the amount of shares being diluted. A company makes the decision to issue new shares because it's a great way to raise insane capital for your company.

After huge hype run ups in mid-2020, GNUS and TOPS decided to issue new shares to take advantage of their good luck. Their company may have eventually sold off and burnt shareholders, but at least they were able to profit from the run up! Make sure to understand that companies can and will take advantage of hype if it comes.

But even companies in bankruptcy will take advantage of their hype moves. After declaring bankruptcy in May 2020, HTZ ran up massively on hype as investors ignored risk and decided the threat of bankruptcy didn't matter to them. It ran up massively and HTZ decided that they'd take advantage of this opportunity by offering more shares. The regulators ultimately rejected this, but this is a solid example of how companies will issue new shares even if it is at the peril of their current shareholders.

HUSN decided it was time to take advantage of their run as well. But they decided to issue new shares at an elevated price only at $0.85, which was substantially higher than their trading price a few months ago but also substantially lower than their current trading price. This contributed greatly to HUSN selling off down to that level after announcement. Be aware of the power of offerings!

## How do we know if a stock is going through a direct offering?

A google news search on the ticker will reveal a direct offering if a company has announced one. But anytime a stock runs up massively (especially in the biotech world as they often need tons of funding) assume that a direct offering announcement can be imminent!
